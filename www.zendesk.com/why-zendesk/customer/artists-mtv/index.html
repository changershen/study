Artists.MTV

A new approach to customer service for a new age of music

Challenge

Back in 1981, one of the most influential icons launched on American cable television. Since then, MTV has influenced generations of artists and become one of the most recognizable brands in the music community. Fast forward 30 years into the digital age, MTV has evolved and broadened far beyond television. To create an online community for artists and their fans, MTV introduced Artists.MTV, where artists create and control their digital presence across MTV networks while getting heard, promoted, and paid through unique MTV artist opportunities.

With the launch of the new platform, Artists.MTV not only needed a support tool to help answer the artists questions, but also one that is easily customizable to fit with MTVs iconic brand.

Zendesk as a solution

MTVs manager of digital music strategy Nathaniel Gravely first came across Zendesk when engaging with Twitters customer service. He immediately saw how fast, effective, and transparent the Zendesk support platform was. As a one-person support team and administrator, Gravely was able to implement Artists.MTVs Zendesk to include:

  • Custom branding: Artist.MTV completely redesigned their Zendesk with custom CSS. In designing the Web portal, Gravely focused on making sure it was intuitive for customers to find self-service and contact supportall the while ensuring it follows the MTV brand.
  • Self-service Web portal: By bucketing common support issues customers, such as account problems, artist page, content, promotions, and payment, Artists.MTV easily funnels customers to the relevant knowledge base content faster.
  • Automated workflows: Starting as a one-person team, Gravely speeds up the support process by getting creative on how they set up triggers, macros, and notifications. For example, Artist.MTV creates many custom tickets that are auto-populated based on tags, so Gravely knows exactly where these tickets are coming from and why the customer is having an issue.
  • Internal collaboration with unlimited light agents: While Gravely may be the only full-time agent and administrator, he has the support of his whole organization. By adding Artist.MTVs tech and media licensing groups as light agents, they can advise and provide expertise on relevant tickets.
  • Ticket sharing between Zendesks: Artists.MTV partners with Topspin Media to develop their product that delivers an interactive and easy-to-manage platform for artists to build their pages. Because Topspin Media also uses Zendesk, Artists.MTV and Topspin Media can rely on each others expertise and easily share and collaborate on tickets to help resolve customer issues togetherall in one seamless communication workflow.

Results

In less than a month, Artists.MTV is already seeing fast adoption both internally and among their customers.

Having Zendesk has absolutely been really important to us during the Artist.MTV launch. Not only were we launching a new product, but we were launching a product that is tied to a huge, reputable brand.

As for whats next for Artists.MTV, Gravely is looking to start building a support team and integrate other networks into their Zendesk, like VH1 and CMT.
